One gesture, three jobs

Select, tap ⌘C C and pick what happens to the text: fix it, summarize it or translate it. Select a link instead, and the reader opens with the whole article.

  1. 1 Select text
  2. 2 Hold ⌘, tap CC
  3. 3 Result at your cursor

Fix

Grammar, spelling and punctuation, without changing the language. A diff shows every change, and a tap reveals the rule behind it.

Summarize

A long paragraph, email or article becomes a short list of the points that matter. Same language as the original.

Translate

English ↔ Polish, German, Russian, Spanish, Dutch, French. You can also leave the second language on automatic — Glosso detects it from the selection.

Getting started

One-time setup, then it becomes a daily reflex with shortcuts at hand.

  1. 1

    Install

    Once, at the start.

    • Download Glosso.zip from Releases, unzip it and drag Glosso.app into Applications.
    • On first launch macOS blocks the app (it's signed, but not notarized by Apple). Click Done, then open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down and click Open Anyway. You only do this once.
    • Grant Accessibility when asked. It's the only permission the app needs.
    • The first-run wizard downloads the model and helps you pick your languages — including the app's primary language, Polish or English.
  2. 2

    Every day

    Shortcuts at hand.

    • ⌘CC open the panel over a selection: fix, summarize or translate.
    • ⌃⌘G fix the selection in place, no panel.
    • ⌃⌘T translate the selection in place.
    • Esc closes an open dropdown first, then the panel. The model and languages live in Settings.

No cloud, no API,
no subscription. Free.

The language model (Gemma via Ollama) runs locally on localhost. No account, no API key, no monthly bill. Whatever you fix or translate never leaves your Mac.

Private

Your text never reaches a server. You can work on a confidential email or a contract and nothing leaks to the cloud.

No subscription

The model lives on your disk. Download it once and use it as much as you like — there's no account to create and no monthly AI bill.

Offline and fast

Works on a plane and without internet. The model stays warm in memory between uses, so results appear instantly.

Copy a link, read it in your language

The same gesture works on a URL. Select a link, tap ⌘C C, and Glosso opens the article in its own window, translated paragraph by paragraph.

  • A summary up front, then the whole text with its images and captions.
  • Translated by the local model, so the article never leaves your Mac.
  • Questions? Ask the article in the chat beside it.
  • Once translated, an article reopens instantly.

Editable source

You can correct the captured text right in the panel and run the model again. There's also a tone switch: automatic, formal, casual.

FIX

She dont doesn't like it.

Why the fix?

Third person singular negates with doesn't: does + not (she doesn't, he doesn't).

A Fix that knows the rules

Every change comes with a reason. Corrections of Polish text cite the actual rules of the Polish Language Council; English ones draw on a base of common mistakes.
